How Our Cheese Is Produced

Explore Our Process Our Types of Cheeses

Introduction

As artisanal cheese makers, we pride ourselves in making cheese by hand, in small quality batches. We see cheese making as an art where particular attention is paid to ethical production practises and environmentally sustainable sourcing methods. Our cheese are produced from the highest quality materials and are free of preservatives and food coloring of any kind.

Milking Stage

Fresh and pure milk is transformed into cheeses of unique taste and textures, revealing subtle range of flavours influenced by the seasons, forage and feed. Each cow is fed a mix of grass and grain farmed from the highest quality crops, giving the cheese an intricate and distinct flavour profile, only Keep It Cheesy can provide. The cows placed into the most comfortable environment, creating ethical milking standards while sourcing the best quality.

Culture Stage

At this crucial and important stage, cheese cultures in the form of specific bacteria strains are added to the milk. The cheese cultures then rapidly raise the acidity of milk by consuming the lactose present within the fresh milk, and converting it into the lactic acid. The cheese curds are then mixed and cut in various shapes and forms, creating the distinct texture that Keep It Cheesy is renowned for.

Aging Stage

After forming the intial stages of the curds, the cheese it then brought down into specially designed cellars that provide the best aging environment to bring our cheeses from great to amazing. Special care is taken into the aging process where we hone our experience and craftsmanship to bring out various notes and profiles that exemplify the complexity that our specialty cheeses are known for.

Packaging & Branding Stage

After we find that the cheeses are producing a very specific yet traditional aroma, the cheese is then shaped and stamped with our seal of quality that shows our loyal consumers that the cheese exemplifies the highest standards here at Keep It Cheesy.

Types of Cheeses

Mild Cheeses

Sourced from young goats, double or triple creams, or bloomy rind cheese.

Medium Cheeses

Cheeses in this category are soft to semi-firm, such as a mild cow, an aged goat, or sheep milk cheese.

Bold Cheeses

Cheeses in this category resemble harder mountain cheeses, long aged cheddars and mild wash rind cheeses (stinky).

Strong Cheeses

Cheeses in this category have a bigger presence and resemble cheeses that have a more assertive washed rind cheese or a classic blue cheese.
