How Are Our Cheese Produced?

Introduction

As artisanal cheese makers, we pride ourselves in making cheese by hand, in small quality batches. We see cheese making as an art where particular attention is paid to ethical production practises and environmentally sustainable sourcing methods. Our cheese are produced from the highest quality materials and are free of preservatives and food coloring of any kind.

Milking Stage

Each day, fresh and pure milk from goats and cows are transformed into cheeses of unique taste and textures, revealing subtle range of flavours influenced by the seasons, forage and feed.

Culture Stage

Each day, fresh and pure milk from goats and cows are transformed into cheeses of unique taste and textures, revealing subtle range of flavours influenced by the seasons, forage and feed.

Aging Stage

Then the cheeses are brought down into specially designed cellars that provide the best aging environment to bring our cheeses from great to amazing. Special care is taken into the aging process where we hone our experience and craftsmanship to bring out various notes and profiles that exemplify the complexity that our specialty cheeses are known for.

Packaging & Branding Stage

The cheese is then shaped and stamped with our seal of quality that shows our loyal consumers that the cheese exemplifies the highest standards here at Keep It Cheesy.

Types of Cheeses

Mild Cheeses

Sourced from young goats, double or triple creams, or bloomy rind cheese.

Pairs well with light white wines and champagnes

Medium Cheeses

Cheeses in this category are soft to semi-firm, such as a mild cow, an aged goat, or sheep milk cheese.

Pairings...

Pairs well with a light red wine, rose, lagers and or pilsner beers

Bold Cheeses

Cheeses in this category resemble harder mountain cheeses, long aged cheddars and mild wash rind cheeses (stinky).

Pairings...

Pairs well with red wine, ales and lambic beers

Strong Cheeses

Cheeses in this category have a bigger presence and resemble cheeses that have a more assertive washed rind cheese or a classic blue cheese.

Pairings...

Pairs well with dessert wines, ports and stout beers.
